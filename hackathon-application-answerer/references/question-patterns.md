# Hackathon Question Patterns

Use this file to classify repeated application prompts by intent and choose a response shape.

## Motivation / Fit

Typical prompts:

- Why do you want to join this hackathon?
- Why are you interested in this track?
- What do you hope to gain?

Response shape:

1. State the specific fit with the theme or community.
2. Connect it to a concrete personal project, skill, or problem.
3. Name what the user will contribute and what they want to learn.

## Background / Bio

Typical prompts:

- Tell us about yourself.
- Describe your technical background.
- What experience do you have?

Response shape:

1. One-sentence identity.
2. Two or three strongest proof points.
3. Current direction or why it matters for this event.

## Project Idea

Typical prompts:

- What do you want to build?
- Describe your hackathon idea.
- What problem are you solving?

Response shape:

1. Name the problem and target user.
2. Describe the solution in plain language.
3. Add differentiator, feasibility, and expected prototype.

## Team Contribution

Typical prompts:

- What can you contribute to a team?
- What role do you usually play?
- What skills are you bringing?

Response shape:

1. State the role clearly.
2. Pair skills with behaviors: build fast, clarify scope, prototype, present, debug, coordinate.
3. Add one proof point from a previous project.

## Impact / Community

Typical prompts:

- How will your project create impact?
- Who benefits?
- Why does this matter?

Response shape:

1. Identify the beneficiary.
2. Explain the current pain or gap.
3. Describe a realistic near-term outcome from the hackathon prototype.

## Challenge / Resilience

Typical prompts:

- Tell us about a challenge you overcame.
- Describe a failure or learning experience.

Response shape:

1. Brief context.
2. Specific obstacle.
3. Action taken.
4. Result and lesson connected to hackathon work.

## Diversity / Perspective

Typical prompts:

- What unique perspective do you bring?
- How would you contribute to the community?

Response shape:

1. Name a perspective grounded in lived experience, discipline, or working style.
2. Show how it affects collaboration or product decisions.
3. Keep it personal and concrete.

## Cross-Disciplinary Direction

Typical prompts:

- What makes you different from other technical applicants?
- What kind of builder do you want to become?
- What fields are you interested in applying technology to?
- How do you combine technical ability with product or user insight?

Response shape:

1. Avoid locking the user into one fixed vertical unless the application requires it.
2. Frame the user as someone with transferable cross-domain ability: entering a new field, understanding people and business workflows, then applying technology to real needs.
3. Mention concrete current anchors when useful: AI+education startup, upcoming AI-enabled headhunting internship, Agent/full-stack development, MVP iteration, user research.
4. Use the method vocabulary when appropriate: make the early domain narrower, analyze pain points, itching points, motivation, resistance, and touchpoints.
5. End with the user's long-term direction: not pure technical execution, but using technology, communication, and demand discovery together to create products that solve concrete problems.
