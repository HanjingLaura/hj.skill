# Output contract

## News card

For each of five stories include: `headline`, `event_date`, `one_sentence_fact`, `why_it_matters`, `primary_source`, `secondary_source` when useful, and `confidence`.

## Fun rewrite

Use four fields: `hook` (under 20 Chinese characters), `setup` (one factual sentence), `punchline` (one clearly comedic sentence), and `fact_anchor` (the fact the joke depends on). Avoid fake quotes.

## Storyboard row

Use: `panel`, `shape`, `visual`, `camera`, `action`, `caption_or_dialogue`, `transition`. Keep dialogue under roughly 25 Chinese characters per bubble and no more than two bubbles per panel.

## Page geometry

- Default canvas: vertical 4:5.
- Produce exactly five pages, one selected news story per page.
- Use 4–7 panels per page with 4–8% outer margin and visible gutters.
- Maintain left-to-right, top-to-bottom reading order.
- Use at least three panel shapes; limit trapezoids/diagonals to emphasis beats so the layout remains readable.
- Put each story's strongest hook in the top 20% and its closing reaction or punchline in the bottom 15%.
- Default to a clean 2-column by 3-row six-panel grid matching a serialized manhua page. Allow one emphasized trapezoid only when it improves drama; do not force irregular panels.

## Default melodrama direction

- Wrap every news item in a complete fictional gossip plot; do not merely add dramatic captions to an explainer.
- Choose a different setting, genre, cast, palette, and visual metaphor according to each story's verified tension. Avoid repeating one template across all five pages.
- Anthropomorphize models, products, companies, or policies as fictional characters; do not depict real executives.
- Use short serialized-drama episode headings and one concise speech bubble per panel when useful, but vary the device when the genre calls for texts, dossiers, rankings, contracts, verdicts, or confession cards.
- Build humor from characters treating real technical facts as emotionally catastrophic relationship drama.
- Prohibit microphones, presenters, reporters, news desks, influencer poses, and direct-to-camera narration by default.

## Image prompt skeleton

Specify: editorial comic style; canvas ratio; exact panel count; numbered panel geometry and location; recurring host appearance; per-panel subjects/actions; unified palette; clean ink; reserved caption and speech-bubble zones; readable visual hierarchy; no logos unless necessary for factual identification.

Negative constraints: no extra panels, no merged panels, no duplicated faces or limbs, no random letters, no illegible dense text, no photorealistic depiction of unverified events, no defamatory visual implication, no watermark.

## Final QA

Check that there are exactly five comic pages and each page covers exactly one selected story. Ensure source facts match the jokes, dates are explicit, humor is not passed off as reporting, panel order is clear, shapes do not obscure text, and no page contains an invented quotation.
