# Evaluation Rubric

Use this reference when analyzing phone notes or recommending next steps.

## Core Dimensions

### Role Motivation

Strong signals:

- Can explain why this role or industry fits their goals.
- Shows curiosity about the actual work, not only the company label.
- Understands tradeoffs such as pressure, repetitive execution, or sales/recruiting nature.

Risks:

- Motivation is vague.
- Treats the role only as a backup.
- Has a mismatch between desired work and actual responsibilities.

### Availability And Stability

Strong signals:

- Clear start date.
- Stable weekly schedule.
- Can meet duration requirements.
- Has thought through commute, school, exams, graduation, or competing offers.

Risks:

- Schedule is uncertain.
- Can only work briefly.
- Has unresolved conflicts with school, exams, travel, or other offers.

### Communication

Strong signals:

- Clear, structured answers.
- Listens and responds directly.
- Can ask sensible questions.
- Comfortable speaking with unfamiliar people.

Risks:

- Avoids direct answers.
- Cannot explain experiences clearly.
- Shows low responsiveness or weak professional etiquette.

### Execution And Resilience

Strong signals:

- Has examples of persistence, pressure handling, or high-volume execution.
- Can describe a real difficulty and how they handled it.
- Shows ownership and follow-through.

Risks:

- Gives only abstract claims.
- Avoids pressure.
- Has frequent unresolved self-doubt or unstable commitment.

### Learning Ability

Strong signals:

- Learns tools quickly.
- Has used AI, data, research, or workflow tools to improve output.
- Can explain how they learn unfamiliar domains.

Risks:

- Low curiosity.
- Cannot describe a learning process.
- Rejects tools or new workflows without reason.

### Recruiting / Sales Orientation

Strong signals:

- Comfortable initiating conversations.
- Interested in people, business, talent, or commercial outcomes.
- Has experience in recruiting, sales, operations, community, research, entrepreneurship, or customer-facing work.

Risks:

- Strongly avoids outreach, rejection, persuasion, or target-driven work.
- Wants a purely back-office or low-interaction role.

## Recommendation Labels

- `advance`: Clear fit, risks manageable, next step should be arranged.
- `leader check`: Promising but needs decision-owner review.
- `hold`: Some fit, but timing or risks make immediate progress uncertain.
- `reject`: Clear mismatch with core requirements.
- `needs more info`: Insufficient evidence; ask follow-up questions.

## Evidence Rule

For every recommendation point or risk point, include the evidence source:

- Resume fact
- Candidate statement
- Call behavior
- Missing information
- Recruiter inference
