# Screening Workflow

Use this reference when preparing candidate screening calls or process handoffs.

## Standard Flow

1. Search / source candidates.
2. Resume screen.
3. Phone screen.
4. Assignment or written task, if required.
5. Internal resume or topic discussion.
6. Leader or hiring manager check.
7. Schedule interview.
8. Pipeline / process list update.
9. Offer or onboarding.

Adapt stage names to the user's private `local/company-context.local.md`.

## Before The Call

- Read the resume or profile carefully.
- Identify why the candidate may fit the role.
- Identify what is unknown or risky.
- Prepare 3-6 tailored questions from the resume.
- Prepare required screening questions from local company context.
- Prepare a concise role pitch that connects the role to the candidate's likely motivation.

## During The Call

Keep the structure predictable:

1. Opening and time check.
2. Short agenda.
3. Company and role introduction.
4. Candidate self-introduction.
5. Resume-based probing.
6. Availability, motivation, and stability check.
7. Candidate questions.
8. Next steps and contact confirmation.

## After The Call

- Convert notes into structured evidence.
- Separate strengths, risks, and unknowns.
- Recommend a next stage.
- Draft a short internal handoff.
- Save reusable questions or strong pitch wording into local context when useful.
